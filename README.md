# Horror Film Analysis

An exploratory data analysis of how horror cinema has evolved from 1970 to 2020.

## Research questions
1. Has horror's popularity (as a share of all films) changed over time?
2. Have horror's ratings changed — and do critics and audiences tell different stories?
3. What factors explain the changes?

## Key findings
- Horror peaked in the 1980s slasher era, collapsed in the 1990s, and has been recovering since
- Critics and audiences diverged sharply in the 2010s — prestige horror (A24 era) elevated critical scores while audience scores hit their lowest point
- Volume, content rating, and production tier all significantly predict ratings — but in different ways for critics vs audiences
- The genre has split into two extremes: prestige indie and DTV/schlock, with major studio horror declining

## Dataset
Rotten Tomatoes Movies Dataset — 17,712 films (Kaggle, Stefano Leone)

## Tools
Python, Pandas, Scipy, Matplotlib, Seaborn
